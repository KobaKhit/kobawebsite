# NBA 3-Point Evolution

The **NBA 3-Point Evolution** represents a significant transformation in basketball strategy, highlighting the increasing importance of the three-point shot in the game. This concept is visualized through an interactive [[nba-3pt-spiral]] that charts the historical progression of three-point shooting from its inception in the ABA during the 1960s to its current dominance in the NBA.

## Historical Context

The three-point line was first introduced in the ABA in 1967, but it wasn't until the NBA adopted it in the 1979-80 season that it began to reshape gameplay. The evolution of three-point shooting has been marked by various phases, including early skepticism, gradual acceptance, and eventual reliance on the three-point shot as a primary scoring method. Key moments, such as the rise of sharpshooters like Steph Curry, have further accelerated this trend, leading to a significant increase in both the attempt and make rates of three-point shots.

## Visualization Features

The interactive spiral visualization allows users to explore the following aspects:

- **Three-Point Attempt Rate**: Displays the percentage of field goal attempts that are three-pointers over the seasons.
- **Make Rate**: Shows the success rate of three-point attempts, providing insight into shooting efficiency trends.
- **Major Inflection Points**: Highlights critical changes in strategy or rule adjustments, such as the analytics revolution led by teams like the Houston Rockets.

This rich data representation not only illustrates the quantitative changes in shooting patterns but also contextualizes them within the broader narrative of basketball evolution.

## Views that Evolved

- Some analysts initially viewed the three-point shot as a low-percentage option, emphasizing mid-range shots instead.
- Over time, the prevailing view shifted to recognize the three-point shot as a critical component of modern basketball strategy, supported by data analytics and performance metrics.
