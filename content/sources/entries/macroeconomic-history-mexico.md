A 16-page paper following the macroeconomic trajectory of Mexico between 1950 and 2010.

## Scope

- Data: [World Bank World Development Indicators](http://data.worldbank.org/data-catalog/world-development-indicators)
- Tools: Excel for data processing and charting
- Coverage: GDP growth, inflation, trade balance, external debt, major crises (1982 debt crisis, 1994 peso crisis)

## Key periods

| Period | Theme |
|---|---|
| 1950–1970 | "Mexican Miracle" — sustained 6–7% GDP growth |
| 1970–1982 | Oil boom and overexpansion |
| 1982 | Debt crisis — default on external debt |
| 1994 | Tequila crisis — peso devaluation |
| 1995–2010 | NAFTA integration and gradual stabilization |
